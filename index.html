History

  • 1921

    Our Humble Beginnings

    The twenty-third Chapter of Pi Kappa Phi, Alpha Gamma, was originally colonized in 1921 by several members of Phi Chapter (University of Tulsa) who transferred to the University of Oklahoma. Colonization lasted two years, while 25 members tried to convince nationals they were worthy of a charter.

  • 1923

    Pi Kappa Phi is Born

    At the 1923 Supreme Chapter meeting in Atlanta, the Alpha Gamma Chapter was granted their charter at the University of Oklahoma, and became the second chapter chartered in the Missouri Valley. During the fall semester of 1923, Alpha Gamma accepted five pledges.

  • 1936

    The Depression

    In 1936, in the midst of the Oklahoma and National Depression, they were forced to relinquish their charter. Many men soon joined the service of their country and a great number continued to serve during WWII.

  • October 31, 1969

    A New Beginning

    Alpha Gamma was recolonized on October 31st, 1969, with seven pledges. The Alpha Gamma Chapter was allowed to rent half of the upstairs of the AGD Sorority house in which they would live. It was the first coed fraternity/sorority living arrangement in the country.

  • 1977

    A House of Our Own

    During the next three years, Alpha Gamma began to acquire a house of their own. The 1977 Pi Kappa did extremely well academically placing fourth in the Greek system for overall GPA. Finances were becoming increasingly important, and they were struggling to afford their house.

  • 1983-1982

    A Rebirth

    During the 1983-1984 school year, many members resigned their memberships and the final chapter President of that era, Jim Gonders, voluntarily withdrew from the house and closed the chapter indefinitely. In 1986, nationals decided to recolonize, though problems were growing. In 1992, everyone was placed on alumni status except for six members who were asked to stay to recolonize.

  • 2008

    The next 16 years saw many ups and downs once more, and in 2008 the Chapter once more surrendered its charter because of inability to meet required national standards.

  • Present Day

    National leaders came back to Norman in 2011 to search for campus leaders, and today, our chapter can attribute so much of what we have and who we are, to the men who worked perpetually to build the ideal brotherhood since 1921. With that said, we are the proud men of Alpha Gamma Chapter, and we will endure.

Spring 2016.

Pi Kappa Phi members at "Donut Let Love Hurt," an Alpha Chi Omega and Sigma Chi philanthropy! "Donut Let Love Hurt" is a donut dash relay and benefit brunch where proceeds are donated towards Sistercare, a battered women and children's shelter.

"Pops with Pi Phi"

Spring 2016.

Pops with Pi Phi is an annual philanthropy event hosted by Pi Beta Phi. Proceeds benefit Read>Lead>Achieve, which help inspire a lifelong love of reading and literacy.

Two Build America particpants (center two), meet their Archon and Vice Archon at the 55th Supreme Chapter. Build America is an event lasting six weeks to promote accessible recreation for people with disabilities.
